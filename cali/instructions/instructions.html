Positioning and Z-index
Required Tools: Google Chrome, Internet, & a text editor
Label two of California's major cities with positioning!
Assignment

Through this handson, you will learn important positioning properties. You will also learn how to use z-index.

Right now, you should have something like this:



Step 1: Align the Labels

Align the labels so that the name of the city is next to a pin. In the name class and in the pin class:

  • set float: 'left'

Step 2: Pin the Cities

If you look closely at your starter file, you'll notice that the left and top properties in your #la-container and your #sf-container have already been filled out. However, it doesn't seem like these containers are in the right place. This is because the position property within these two containers is still 'static' (by default) and so the positioning properties have no effect. To fix this, add:

  • position: 'absolute' to #la-container and #sf-container

Step 3: Get the Details

Now all the locations should be marked. However, the text and the pins are way too close together and the text is split into two lines. Fix this by adding:

  • position: 'absolute' in the .name class, and
  • specify the dimensions in the .label-container class (height: '40px'; width: '500px')

Step 4: Cover the Tree

Right now, a huge tree is covering our text. Fix that by changing the z-index of the tree.

  • z-index: "-1" in #tree
